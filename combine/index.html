    What does combining fonts do?

    Every style on the main generator transforms plain text once. The Combiner feeds each step's output into the next step as its input — the same thing you'd get by copying a result and pasting it back in, but live. Some combinations produce text that no single style can: flip your words upside-down and then underline them, and every inverted letter carries its own underline — ʇ̲x̲ǝ̲ʇ̲ ʎ̲ɔ̲u̲ɐ̲ɟ̲.

    Why do some steps say “no change”?

    Most alphabet styles (bold, script, fraktur…) only know how to transform ordinary letters A–Z. Once a step has replaced those with special Unicode characters — say, 𝐛𝐨𝐥𝐝 letters — a second alphabet style has nothing left to transform and passes the text through untouched. That's what the no change badge means. Effects are different: underlines, strikethroughs and glitch marks are combining characters that attach to any letter, so they stack happily on top of whatever came before. The rule of thumb: at most one alphabet style (usually first), then as many effects as you like.

    Will the combined text paste anywhere?

    Yes — the result is still just Unicode text, exactly like the single styles, so it works in Instagram bios, Discord, TikTok, X and nearly any text field. The more marks you stack, the more you depend on each platform's fonts rendering combining characters cleanly, so very heavy stacks (especially zalgo) can look different from app to app. See where fancy text works for the platform-by-platform picture.
