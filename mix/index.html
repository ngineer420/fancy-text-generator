How does per-letter styling work?

Each style on the main generator is really a lookup table of Unicode characters — a bold 𝐚, a gothic 𝔞, a bubble ⓐ are all separate real characters. That means styles don't have to apply to whole strings: this tool keeps a style per letter and swaps each character for its counterpart in whatever alphabet you painted onto it. The result — something like 𝕱a̶n̶ₙcy Tₑₓₜ — is still ordinary text you can paste into any bio, chat or caption.

Why do some letters barely change?

Not every stylized letter exists in Unicode. Subscript is missing most letters, small caps has no q or x, and several styles have no digits. When a painted style has no character for that letter, fontloom falls back to the plain letter rather than showing a broken box — so a few style/letter combinations look identical to plain text. A quick accessibility note

Mixed-font text is the hardest flavor of fancy text for screen readers, which may announce each styled letter separately ("mathematical fraktur capital F…"). Great for a display name, less kind in the middle of a paragraph — see fancy text and screen readers for simple rules of thumb.
